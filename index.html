Honey Butter Cookies

Optimized Home Batch

20–24 yield 350°F ~45 min Soft & chewy

Added by Caleb

Honey Butter Cookies
Yield 20–24
Temp 350°F
Texture Soft center

Ingredients

Unsalted butter12 tbsp (170g)
Good-quality honey½ cup (170g)
Granulated sugar¼ cup (50g)
Light brown sugar, packed¼ cup (55g)
Egg yolks (room temp)2 large
Vanilla extract2 tsp
All-purpose flour2¼ cups (280g)
Baking soda½ tsp
Fine salt½ tsp
Milk powder (optional)2 tsp
Flaky salt for toppingOptional

Method

1

Brown butter. Cook over medium heat until milk solids are golden and nutty. Cool until soft but not liquid.

2

Cream. Beat browned butter with both sugars 1–2 min until smooth. Don't overwhip.

3

Add liquids. Mix in honey (slow stream), egg yolks, vanilla. Beat until glossy and emulsified.

4

Add dry. Whisk flour, soda, salt, milk powder. Fold in just until no dry streaks remain.

5

Chill. Rest 10 min at room temp, then refrigerate — 30 min minimum, overnight for best flavor.

6

Portion. Scoop ~2 tbsp balls (40–45g). Space 2–3" apart on lined light-colored sheet pan.

7

Bake. 11–14 min at 350°F. Pull when edges set, tops matte, centers slightly soft.

8

Finish. Flaky salt immediately if using. Cool 5 min on pan, then rack.

Calibration Notes

Thicker

Chill longer · bake from cold · taller mounds

Chewier

+1 tbsp brown sugar · underbake slightly

Crisper edges

+1–2 min bake · flatten dough first

More honey

Orange blossom honey · swap 1 tbsp sugar for honey

Storage

Room temp airtight 3–4 days
Fridge dough Up to 3 days
Frozen dough balls 2 months · bake frozen +2 min

Chef's Note

To push these toward elite bakery texture, the next meaningful levers are flour protein content, butter moisture variability, and controlled pan heat — all surprisingly influential for a cookie this simple.

Banana Bread

3-Banana Classic — Beat Bake Eat

1 loaf 9×5 pan ~50 min Moist & tender

Added by Darcy

Banana Bread
Yield 1 loaf
Pan 9×5 in
Bake 45–50 min

Ingredients

Overripe bananas, mashed3
Granulated sugar¾ cup
Baking soda1 tsp
Ground cinnamon1 tsp
Salt½ tsp
Vegetable oil⅓ cup
Eggs2 large
Vanilla extract1 tsp
All-purpose flour1¼ cups

Method

1

Combine wet base. In a large bowl, mix mashed bananas, sugar, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt until well combined.

2

Add liquids. Beat in vegetable oil, eggs, and vanilla until incorporated.

3

Add flour. Fold in flour until just incorporated. Do not overmix.

4

Pan. Pour batter into a greased or parchment-lined 9×5 inch loaf pan.

5

Bake. Bake at 350°F for 30 min, then loosely tent with foil. Continue baking 15–20 min until a toothpick in the center comes out clean.

6

Cool. Cool in pan 10 min, then transfer to a rack to cool completely.

Chef's Note

The foil tent at 30 min is the key move — it keeps the crust from over-darkening while the center finishes. Blacker the bananas, sweeter the bread.

Lemon-Blueberry Dutch Baby

Cast Iron Skillet

Serves 2–4 450°F 15 min Puffy & golden

Added by Darcy

Lemon-Blueberry Dutch Baby
Serves 2–4
Temp 450°F
Bake 15 min

Batter (blend all)

Eggs4 large
All-purpose flour½ cup
Milk½ cup
Sugar3 T
Butter, melted2 T
Vanilla extract1 tsp
Salt¼ tsp
Lemon or lime zest (or juice)1 whole / 1–2 T

Pan & Toppings

Butter (for pan)2 T
Fresh blueberries1 cup
Powdered sugarto taste
Maple syrupdrizzle

Method

1

Blend batter. Combine eggs, flour, milk, sugar, melted butter, vanilla, salt, and zest in blender. Blend until smooth.

2

Preheat. Heat oven to 450°F. Melt 2 T butter in cast iron skillet over medium heat until foamy.

3

Add berries. Scatter 1 cup blueberries evenly into the buttered skillet.

4

Pour & bake. Pour batter over blueberries. Transfer immediately to oven. Bake 15 min until dramatically puffed and golden at the edges.

5

Serve. It will sink while cooling — that's expected. Dust with powdered sugar, drizzle maple syrup. Serve straight from the skillet.

Chef's Note

The dramatic puff happens fast — have everyone at the table before you pull it from the oven. Zest gives a brighter citrus hit than juice; use both for maximum flavor.
